In the vast ocean of free-to-play games on Steam, some titles shine brightly, yet many remain hidden gems waiting to be discovered. One such game is "12, Memory Lane," which has garnered "Very Positive" reviews since its release on December 15, 2025. This engaging narrative-driven experience invites players to explore the abandoned estate of Henry Villemaine, piecing together the mystery of his family's disappearance through journals and documents.

"12, Memory Lane" is a short but impactful game, clocking in at just under two hours. While it may be categorized as a walking simulator, it offers a rich tapestry of puzzles and exploration that keeps players invested. Developed by students at Rubika Supinfogame under the name Forget-Me-Not Studio, this title exemplifies the potential of indie games to deliver emotionally resonant stories without the price tag.

Despite some critiques regarding its controls and length, the overall reception has been overwhelmingly positive. As the gaming landscape continues to evolve, titles like "12, Memory Lane" remind us of the incredible experiences that can be found in free games. Will you add this captivating adventure to your library and uncover the secrets of the Villemaine family?
