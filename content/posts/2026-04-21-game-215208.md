Hello Kitty creator Sanrio is making a significant leap into the gaming industry, launching its own brand, Sanrio Games. This strategic move aims to capitalize on the booming global games market by integrating its beloved characters into interactive experiences. With plans to release ten titles over the next three years, including a party game for Nintendo Switch, Sanrio is poised to expand its reach and revenue streams in a competitive landscape.

The gaming initiative reflects a broader trend among Japanese intellectual property owners who are diversifying their revenue by extending popular franchises into interactive entertainment. Sanrio's CEO, Tomokuni Tsuji, emphasized the potential of games to enhance their licensing business, particularly in the North American market. As the company invests approximately 10 billion yen in development and marketing, the success of these titles could significantly impact its overall performance.

As Sanrio ventures into this new territory, the key challenge will be leveraging its rich portfolio of characters to create compelling gaming experiences. With a Hello Kitty movie set for global release in 2028, fans are eager to see how these beloved characters will adapt to the gaming world. Will Sanrio's gamble pay off, and can they create the next big hit in gaming?
